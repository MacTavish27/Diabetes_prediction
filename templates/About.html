About diabetes

Diabetes mellitus is a group of metabolic disorders characterized by hyperglycemia, which is the result of defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both. Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels over a prolonged period. The condition develops due to the body’s inability to produce enough insulin or effectively use the insulin it produces. Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that regulates blood sugar levels by allowing glucose to enter the body’s cells to be used as energy.
